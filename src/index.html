Inquests in limbo

Inquests into killings at the hands of police have been stalled for years due to legal challenges. While an estimated 36 cases are in line for an inquest in King County, the cases of these six people killed by police are officially designated as the countyâ€™s current pending inquests.

Damarius Butts

Shot April 20, 2017, by Seattle police

Butts was shot and killed by Seattle police following a report of an armed robbery at a downtown convenience store. Nineteen-year-old Butts and his 17-year-old sister reportedly stole doughnuts, chips and a 12-pack of beer, showing the clerk a handgun on the way out. Officers gave chase and cornered him at the downtown Federal Office Building, where gunshots were exchanged. A police officer was struck in the protective vest with a round. Butts suffered multiple gunshot wounds.

Isaiah Obet

Shot June 10, 2017, by Auburn police

Obet, 25, was shot and killed by Auburn police Officer Jeffrey Nelson, who was responding to a call of a man acting strangely and possibly armed with a small knife. Nelson said when he confronted Obet, the man ran and tried to open the door of a nearby vehicle. Nelson, a K-9 officer, released his dog, which attacked Obet. Nelson then shot Obet twice, once in the chest and once in the head. Last year, the city settled a lawsuit brought by the family for $1.25 million. Shortly after, it was announced that Nelson, who has an extensive history of using force, was charged with murder in the death of Jesse Sarey.

Charleena Lyles

Shot June 18, 2017, by Seattle police

Lyles, a pregnant, 30-year-old mother of four, was shot seven times by two Seattle police officers on June 18, 2017. She had called 911 to report a burglary at her Northeast Seattle apartment. Officers Steven McNew and Jason Anderson said Lyles, who had a history of mental illness and previous run-ins with police, suddenly threatened them with a kitchen knife after they entered her apartment. No evidence of a burglary was found.

Eugene Nelson

Shot Aug. 9, 2017, by Kent police

Nelson, 20, from Seattle, was killed by Kent police after he showed up at a strip mall in violation of a domestic-violence no-contact order filed by his girlfriend. Nelson, who was also wanted for car theft, reportedly dragged a police officer and his K-9 partner with his vehicle before two officers fired into his car, killing him.

Robert Lightfeather

Shot Oct. 30, 2017, by Federal Way police

Lightfeather, 33, a member of the Bois Forte Band of the Lake Superior Chippewa Tribe, was shot and killed by Federal Way police after reportedly threatening two men with a gun along Pacific Highway South. Two officers said they fired when Lightfeather pointed the gun at them.

Curtis Elroy Tade

Shot Dec. 19, 2017, by Kirkland police

Tade, 47, was shot and killed when Kirkland police responded to the Emerson Apartments after neighbors reported hearing screams and possible gunshots. Tade, who was reportedly carrying an assault rifle, was shot by at least one officer and died at the scene from multiple gunshot wounds.
