Inquests in limbo

Inquests into killings at the hands of police have been stalled for years due to legal challenges. While an estimated 36 cases are in line for an inquest in King County, the cases of these six people killed by police are officially designated as the countyâ€™s current pending inquests.

Damarius Butts

Shot April 20, 2017 by Seattle police

Butts was shot and killed by Seattle police on April 20, 2017, following a report of an armed robbery at a downtown convenience store in which the 19-year-old Butts and his 17-year-old sister reportedly stole doughnuts, chips and a 12-pack of beer, showing the clerk a handgun on the way out of the story. Officers gave chase and cornered Butts at the downtown Federal building, where gunshots were exchanged. A police officer was struck in the protective vest with a round. Butts, who is Black, suffered multiple gunshot wounds.

Isaiah Obet

Shot June 10, 2017 by Auburn police

Obet, 25 was shot and killed by Auburn Police Officer Jeffrey Nelson on June 10, 2017, who was responding to a call of a man acting strangely, possibly armed with a small knife. Nelson confronted Obet, where Nelson said the man ran from him and tried to open the door of a nearby vehicle. Nelson, a K-9 officer, released his dog, which attacked Obet. Nelson then shot Obet twice, once in the chest and once in the head. The city settled a lawsuit filed by the family for $1.25 million after Nelson, an officer, with an extensive history of using force, was charged with the murder of Jesse Sarey last year.

Charleena Lyles

Shot June 18, 2017 by Seattle police

Lyles, a pregnant 30-year-old Black mother of four, was shot seven times by two white Seattle police officers on June 18, 2017, after she had called 911 to report a burglary at her Northeast Seattle apartment. The officers said Lyles, who had a history of mental illness and previous run-ins with police, suddenly threatened Officers Steven McNew and Jason Anderson with one or two kitchen knives after they entered her apartment. No evidence of a burglary was found.

Eugene Nelson

Shot Aug. 9, 2017 by Kent police

Nelson, 20, from Seattle, was killed Aug. 9, 2017, by Kent police after he showed up at a strip mall in violation of a domestic-violence no-contact order filed by his girlfriend. Also wanted for car theft, Nelson reportedly dragged a police officer and his K-9 partner with his vehicle before two officers fired into his car, killing him.

Robert Lightfeather

Shot Oct. 30, 2017 by Federal Way police

Lightfeather, 33, a member of the Bois Fort Band of the Lake Superior Chippewa Tribe, was shot and killed by Federal Way police on Oct. 30, 2017, after reportedly threatening two men with a gun along Pacific Highway South. Two officers said they fired when Lightfeather pointed the gun at them.

Curtis Elroy Tade

Shot Dec. 19, 2017 by Kirkland police

Tade, 47, was shot and killed Dec. 19, 2017, after Kirkland police reportedly responded to the Emerson Apartments after neighbors reported hearing screams and possible gunshots. Tade, who was reportedly carrying an assault rifle, was shot by at least one officer and died at the scene from multiple gunshot wounds.
